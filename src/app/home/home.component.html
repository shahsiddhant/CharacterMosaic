There has never been a more important time than now to embrace and affirm those among us who so often have been made to be invisible.

The Character Mosaic Project grew out of the need for a more diverse pool of free character rigs for use in animated films. When searching on the Internet for well built rigs to use, in Autodesk's Maya, that were non-Caucasian males, what we found was that minorities were severely under represented (see image below). With that void of minority representation, there becomes a adverse effect on the number of films and games that are not made with minority characters in them.

In the past students have taken the Caucasian characters and changed the texture of their skin. The problem is facial bone structure, clothing styles, and accessories are not the same for the diversity in culture around the world. Changing the texture on a Caucasian male character ends up usually looking like a Caucasian character with a different skin color. This project aims to change that dynamic starting with eight characters.

Currently we are in the beginning stages of this project. We have a contest open to Rochester Institute of Technology students to find the character designs that we will use to create the fully rigged 3D characters.
